title: Is the CS Job Market Really Cooked?
---

If you spend any time online, it’s easy to feel like the CS job market is completely hopeless. This is 90% cope that people aren't where they want to be, and its easy to join in and blame the market instead of working hearder. 

---

# Where this idea comes from

A lot of the fear around the job market comes from:
- layoffs at big tech companies  
- more people studying CS  
- competitive recruiting cycles  

These are real things — but they get exaggerated online.

What starts as “it’s more competitive right now” turns into:
> “there are no opportunities left”

Which just isn’t accurate.

---

# Myth #1: “There are no jobs in tech anymore”

There are still:
- startups hiring  
- mid-size companies growing  
- teams that need engineers  

The difference is that:
👉 it’s not as easy as it used to be

That’s not the same as impossible.

---

# What’s actually changed

The biggest shift isn’t that jobs disappeared — it’s that:

- companies are more selective  
- hiring is slower  
- expectations are a bit higher  

That means:
- you might need to apply more  
- you might need to prepare more intentionally  

But that’s very different from “there’s no point trying.”

---

# Why fear-mongering is harmful

When people constantly say things like:
- “it’s over”
- “don’t even try”
- “you’re too late”

It doesn’t help anyone.

It just:
- discourages people who are capable  
- creates unnecessary stress  
- makes the process feel worse than it already is  

---

## 🌱 What actually matters more

Instead of focusing on how “bad” the market is, it’s more useful to focus on:

- building real skills  
- understanding how recruiting actually works  
- being consistent with applications  
- improving over time  

Those things matter in *any* market.

---

## ✨ Final thoughts

The CS job market isn’t perfect right now.

But it’s also not “cooked.”

It’s just more competitive — and a little less forgiving.

And if you’re willing to keep learning, improving, and showing up consistently, there’s still space for you.
