## the prestige trap in tech

There’s this weird thing that happens once you start studying computer science. At some point, without anyone explicitly saying it, you start to believe that a big tech offer is proof of success.  

You start thinking
> “I need a job that proves something”

---

## the problem with that mindset

The issue isn’t that FAANG jobs are bad, its that you're doing yourself a disservice if you're in it for the money.


Which leads to:
- people feeling like failures for getting perfectly good jobs  
- comparing yourself constantly to a tiny percentage of students  
- ignoring opportunities that might actually fit you better  

It turns your entire career into a ranking system.

---

## 🧠 what people don’t say enough

Most people do not end up at FAANG.

And they’re still:
- making good money  
- building interesting things  
- living completely normal, successful lives  

There are:
- startups  
- mid-size companies  
- finance/tech roles  
- internal tools teams  
- niche but high-impact roles  

that people barely talk about… but are actually really solid.

---

## how it messes with your perspective

The more you’re in tech spaces online, the more distorted things get.

It starts to feel like:
- everyone is getting insane offers  
- everyone is grinding 24/7  
- everyone is aiming for the same 5 companies  

But that’s just not reality.

It’s just what gets posted.

---

## 💡 what success actually looks like (for most people)

For most people, success in tech is:

- having a stable, well-paying job  
- working on things that are at least somewhat interesting  
- having time for a life outside of work  
- growing over time  

Not:
- optimizing every decision for prestige  
- chasing logos  
- constantly feeling behind  

---

## where I’m at with it

I’m still figuring this out.

It’s hard not to compare yourself when that’s the environment you’re in.

But I’m starting to realize that:

just because something is the most talked about path  
doesn’t mean it’s the best one
