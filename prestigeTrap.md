# Prestige Trap & How to Break Free

You need to have the courage to chase the goals you have and not the goals of others. 

In computer science theres this toxic idea that the pinnacle of success and status is an offer at a FAANG company. Its not for the reasons you think.

You start thinking 
> “I need a job that proves something”

## Whats the Cost? 

This is where it gets dangerous. Because once you start thinking like that, your goals stop being yours.

You’re not choosing paths based on curiosity, or interest, or even lifestyle — you’re choosing based on what sounds impressive when you say it out loud.

And the worst part is, it *works*.  
People will be impressed.  
You’ll get validation.

But validation is a really weak foundation for a career.

If your decisions are driven by external approval, you end up optimizing for optics instead of reality. You pick roles that look good, not ones that feel right. You chase logos instead of learning.

And that’s how you wake up one day in a job you worked so hard to get… and realize you don’t even like it.
