# How to Get a Software Engineering Internship in 2027  
> *what actually works (and what doesn’t)*

Getting a software engineering internship feels random.

Some people apply to 200+ roles and get nothing.  
Others get offers early and make it look easy.

So what actually matters?

Because if you listen to everything online, you’ll end up trying to do *everything*:
- 10 projects  
- 500 LeetCode problems  
- perfect GPA  
- hackathons, clubs, networking  

And somehow, it still doesn’t feel like enough.

---

## What People Think Matters

There’s a checklist everyone follows:
- build a bunch of projects  
- grind LeetCode endlessly  
- apply everywhere  
- hope something works  

None of these things are wrong.

But the problem is how people approach them.

Most people optimize for *doing more*, not doing what actually works.

So they:
- build random projects they don’t understand  
- spam applications with the same resume  
- do LeetCode without learning patterns  

And then wonder why nothing changes.

---

## What Actually Matters

If you break the process down, getting a SWE internship comes down to two things:

1. **Getting interviews**  
2. **Passing interviews**

That’s it.

Everything you do should help one of those.

---

### Getting Interviews

This is where most people fail.

Not because they’re not good enough—but because their resume doesn’t show it.

What actually matters:
- **Clarity > complexity**  
- Your projects should be easy to understand in 10 seconds  
- Use real keywords (APIs, databases, tools)

You don’t need 5 projects.

You need 1–2 that:
- actually work  
- you can explain clearly  
- sound like something a company would care about  

Also:

Referrals matter more than people admit.

You don’t need a crazy network—just:
- classmates  
- alumni  
- people at companies you’re applying to  

---

### Passing Interviews

This is where LeetCode comes in.

Yes—LeetCode still matters.

But not in the way people think.

You don’t need 500 problems.

What you actually need:
- pattern recognition  
- consistency  
- the ability to explain your thinking  

Doing 100–150 problems *well* is better than doing 500 randomly.

Focus on:
- arrays, hashmaps  
- trees, graphs  
- basic dynamic programming  

And most importantly:
👉 **practice explaining your solution out loud**

Because interviews aren’t just coding—they’re communication.

---

## The Real Bottleneck

Most people think they’re failing because they’re not “good enough.”

But usually it’s one of these:

- they’re not getting interviews  
- they’re studying inefficiently  
- they’re spreading themselves too thin  

So they respond by doing more… instead of doing better.

---

## A Realistic Plan

If you want something concrete, here’s what actually works:

### Month 1–2
- Build 1–2 solid projects  
- Fix your resume (make it clear, not fancy)

### Month 3–4
- Start LeetCode (focus on patterns)  
- Practice explaining solutions

### Month 5+
- Apply consistently  
- Reach out for referrals  
- Do mock interviews  

That’s it.

No need to overcomplicate it.

---

## The Truth No One Says

Getting a software engineering internship isn’t about doing everything.

It’s about doing the *right things consistently*.

A lot of people get stuck because they’re chasing what *looks* impressive instead of what actually works.

And that’s how you end up spending months grinding… with nothing to show for it.

---

If you’re going to put in that much effort,  
make sure it’s going in the right direction.
