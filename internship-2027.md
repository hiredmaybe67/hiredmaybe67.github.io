Everywhere online will tell you theres no internships to be gotten for summer 2027 but thats not the case. 

My sophomore year, I recived at least 300 rejections until I finally got one in late April. It was barley even software engineering and I spent most of my time on PowerApps and Excel. The next summer I got 3 big tech interviews and 1 offer. For full time, I landed 6 big tech interviews.

### Getting Interviews

This is where most people fail. Not because they’re not good enough—but because their resume doesn’t show it.

What actually matters:
- **Clarity > complexity**  
- Your projects should be easy to understand in 10 seconds
- Use real keywords (APIs, databases, tools)
  
Make a mix of techincal and non-technical people look it over and make sure they can understand your role and the impact of it. You don’t need 5 projects. Honestly don't waste your time on complex projects (do more Leetcode, I swear thats where your time is best spent). You need 1–2 that you can explain, and I've never been asked about them in an interview. I would only imagine they are asked about when you have no other techinical experiance. 

## Leetcode

People will tell you Leetcode style interviews and OA's are going away, but thats not true. Do Leetcode every day and get amazing at it. Its the skill that will get you to past the OA phase and land you a shot at an interview. Your resume and Leetcode skills are what is going to get you an interview and need to be mastered. Leetcode Preimum is worth it!!!


## Apply Early and to EVERYTHING

I made the mistake of only applying to companies that I recognized my first recruiting season. Apply to everything, the worst case scenerio is that you get low-stakes interview practice.
